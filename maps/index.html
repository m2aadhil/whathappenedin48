Maps & Data

The demographic transformation of Palestine — seen through maps, numbers, and the villages that disappeared.

The most powerful way to understand what happened in 1948 is to watch the map change. Three maps tell the story — before the partition, the partition itself, and what actually happened by 1949.

In 1946, Palestine's population was roughly 67% Arab and 33% Jewish. The UN Partition Plan allocated 56% of the land to the proposed Jewish state. By the end of the war, Israel controlled 78% of Mandatory Palestine — and hundreds of villages had been erased from the map.

Land Ownership in 1947

At the time of the UN Partition Plan, Jews owned approximately 7% of the land in Mandatory Palestine. The remainder was owned by Palestinian Arabs, held communally by villages, or classified as state land inherited from the Ottoman and British administrations.
