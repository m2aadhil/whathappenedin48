This Is a Documentation Project

In 1948, more than 700,000 Palestinians were displaced from their homes. Four hundred villages were destroyed. The state of Israel was born. A refugee crisis began that now enters its eighth decade.

Most people have heard fragments of this story. Few have seen the full picture. We exist to close that gap.

What Happened in '48? is not an advocacy organization. We do not tell you what to think, what to support, or what to oppose. We collect, verify, and present evidence — original documents, demographic data, historical maps, survivor testimonies, and legal rulings — and we let it speak for itself.

The editorial principle of this project is simple: We don't argue. We document.

If you came here skeptical, you're in the right place. Skepticism is healthy. We invite you to apply it to everything you see here — including our own work. Check the sources. Read the footnotes. Look at the maps. Listen to the testimonies.

The rest is up to you.
