Voices

History told by the people who experienced it. Refugee testimonies, Israeli historians, and eyewitness accounts of 1948.

History is not just dates and statistics. It is human beings — with names, faces, homes, and keys they still carry. This section collects the voices of those who lived through 1948, those who documented it, and those who have re-examined it.

Refugee Stories

Every Palestinian refugee family has a story. More than 700,000 were displaced — and each displacement is a specific moment: the sound of approaching gunfire, the decision to flee or stay, the key to a house never returned to.

Coming soon: Curated testimonies from the Nakba oral history projects, with transcripts, audio, and photographs. We are working with the Palestinian Oral History Archive and other documentation projects to bring these stories to this site.

Israeli Historians: The New Historians

In the late 1980s, a group of Israeli scholars — including Benny Morris, Ilan Pappé, Avi Shlaim, and Tom Segev — gained access to newly declassified Israeli state archives. What they found challenged the official Israeli narrative of 1948.

They documented that:

  • Many Palestinian refugees were expelled, not merely "encouraged to leave"
  • Israel bore significant responsibility for the refugee crisis
  • The 1948 war included massacres of civilians
  • Arab leaders did not order Palestinians to flee en masse, as had been claimed

These scholars came to be known as the "New Historians." Their work — documented, peer-reviewed, and based on Israeli state archives — remains the most credible account of what happened.*

Voices of Conscience

Not all Israeli voices have defended the events of 1948. From the philosopher Martin Buber, who protested the Deir Yassin massacre in 1948, to contemporary organizations like Breaking the Silence and B'Tselem, many Israelis have spoken out against the treatment of Palestinians — before, during, and after 1948.

These voices matter. They demonstrate that this is not a question of Jewish versus Arab — but of power, land, and human rights.

* Morris, Benny. The Birth of the Palestinian Refugee Problem, 1947–1949 (1988) and Revisited (2004). Pappé, Ilan. The Ethnic Cleansing of Palestine (2006). Shlaim, Avi. The Iron Wall (2001). Segev, Tom. 1949: The First Israelis (1986).

We don't argue. We document.

What Happened in '48 is a non-advocacy documentation project. We collect, verify, and present evidence — and let it speak for itself.
